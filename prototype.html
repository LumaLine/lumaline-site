Our Solar-Powered Train Prototype

The LumaLine prototype is a small-scale solar-powered train built to demonstrate how renewable energy can efficiently power rail systems. Our model includes a photovoltaic panel mounted on the roof to capture solar energy, which is then stored in a battery system that powers the motor.

The design simulates real-world energy cycles and mimics onboard energy conversion and storage systems that would be required in full-size trains. The key mathematical and physical concepts include energy conversion efficiency, power output calculations (P = IV), battery capacity estimation, and kinematic modeling of train motion under load.

This prototype proves that a larger-scale implementation of solar trains is not only possible but also practical — especially in a sun-rich country like Saudi Arabia. With adequate investment and engineering, solar trains can reduce national reliance on fossil fuels, lower emissions, and support the Vision 2030 and the Saudi Green Initiative goals.
