Our Solar-Powered Train Prototype

The LumaLine prototype showcases a scaled version of our solar-powered train concept. We engineered this model to demonstrate how solar panels can power electric motors efficiently and sustainably.

Simplified Diagram of the Energy Flow in the LumaLine Train

Using photovoltaic cells, stored energy charges onboard batteries which drive the motor and wheels. We calculated the torque, force, and energy transfer using the following physics equations:

\[ P = V \times I \quad \text{(Electrical Power)} \]
\[ F = ma \quad \text{(Newton's Second Law)} \]
\[ \tau = r \times F \quad \text{(Torque)} \]
\[ E = P \times t \quad \text{(Energy Used)} \]

These formulas allowed us to optimize the weight-to-power ratio and efficiency. We also modeled the solar energy output:

\[ E_{solar} = A \cdot r \cdot H \cdot PR \quad \text{where:} \]
\[ A = \text{panel area}, \; r = \text{panel efficiency}, \; H = \text{solar irradiance}, \; PR = \text{performance ratio} \]
